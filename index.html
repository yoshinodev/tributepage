Admiral Grace Murray Hopper

The Esteemed Computer Scientist (Pioneer).

Some of the great achievements time line of Grace Hopper's life:

  • 1906 - Hopper was born in New York City.
  • 1934 - Earned a PhD in mathematics from Yales.
  • 1943 - Hopper enlisted in the US Navy, graduated first in her class.
  • 1944
    1. - Helped build the first computer in america (MarkI).
    2. - Made the atomic bomb work
  • 1947 - Grace was the original "Debugger". She and her team coined the therm "debugging" a computer, when they literally had to pull a dead moth out of the Mark II computer.
  • 1949 - The first electronic digital computer produced for commercial use.
  • 1951 - 1952 - Helped Invent Software by creating the first compiler ever developed for an electronic computer.
  • 1959 - "Amazing Grace", as she was known, also helped invent coding language COBOL, which became the primary programming language for business applications.
  • 1966 - 1973 - Retired and called to return to Naval Duty, twice. Right then, she was promoted to rank of Captain with her eight military awards.
  • 1986 - Retires from Navy again at age of 79 with rank of Rear Admiral - Lower Half.
  • 1992 - Admiral Grace M. Hopper dies.
